Intro

The week of October 7th is probably my busiest week during the term. I had a midterm, a presentation and a few deliverables for the cloud computing course. Now that I have survived the week, I want to know how I spent the week using mobile data collected by the aware framework.

Visual Narrative

Location Visualization

The map visualization shows where I had been using the location data. The markers in the map represent my recorded locations. The size of the marker corresponds to how long I had been in one location, and the color corresponds to my speed when the location was recorded. The map visualization shows that I had been commuting between my home and the school exclusively during the week. The biggest bubble in the map is the location of my home. The bubbles at the campus show that I spent a lot of my time in the Gates Hillman complex, and I also spent some time in the Porter Hall, where I take Machine Learning with Large Dataset. I usually walk home via the Fair Oaks street, but the map visualization shows a path from the school to home via the Beacon street scattered with darker bubbles, this is the result of me taking the shuttle service on October 20th.

App Usage Visualization

The app usage visualization shows how much time I spent on each app during the week. The size of the bubble represents the amount of time I spent on the apps. I spent a lot of time on WeChat, which is a communication app like Facebook Messenger. Interestingly, I also spent a fairly large amount of time on "抖音短视频" (Tik Tok), which is a platform to share 15-second videos. I must have been consuming Tik Toks during my breaks. I also spent some time on Uber Eats and Amazon Shopping. I was too busy during the week to do grocery shopping in a store and prepare meals.

Significant Movement Visualization

The Movements line plot shows the number of significant movements during the week. There are spikes of large movements on October 7th and October 8th, especially in the afternoon of October 8th. I was practicing a presentation with my partner, and I was standing up and moving around during the practice sessions, which must be the reason of the huge spikes. I had a midterm on the afternoon of October 9th, and I was studying and printing the cheat sheet in the last minute since I spent all my time preparing my presentation on Monday, and I was rushing towards the exam location, which was captured by the spikes on October 9th. I spent the rest of the week doing my machine learning and cloud computing homework at home and the library, so there were no spikes from October 10th to 14th.
